About Me

Hi! I am a PhD student in Ecology and Evolutionary biology at the University of Michigan. My main research interests are in the field of microbial community ecology and include patterns of community composition, evolutionary ecology, and integrating bacteria into current ecological theories. For my dissertation work evaluated these questions in freshwater systems. I hope to receive my PhD in August 2018 and I am currently looking for a post doctoral position in microbial ecology and evolution.

In addition to my dissertation research on freshwater bacterial communities, I am graduate student consultant through the Center for Research on Learning and Teaching, the co-chair of the mentoring subcommittee of the globally distributed Software Carpentry organization, and an executive board member of the University of Michigan chapter of FEMMES.
