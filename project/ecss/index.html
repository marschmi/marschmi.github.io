Early Career Scientist Committee (ECSS)

I was one of two graduate student representitives for the Early Career Scientist Symposium (ECSS) organizing committees in 2015 and 2016. ECSS is an annual one-day symposium hosted by my graduate department, the Department of Ecology & Evolutionary Biology at the University. The goal of the symposium is to brings together members of the University of Michigan community with scientist who work off campus on a current ‘hot topic’. Every year the topic of the symposium changes. When I served on the committee in 2015, the topic was the “Ecosystems within organisms: Ecology and Evolution of the Microbiome” while in 2016, the topic was “Frontiers in Community Assembly”.

For each symposium, we invited 2 late career speakers and 7 early career speakers who were either a PhD student, post-doc, or early in their professorial career. For the early career speakers, there was an application process. As part of my position on the committee, I helped to select applicants, organize the order of the speakers, announce the speakers, coordinate the poster session, and facilitate the end of day discussion.
