Teaching Consulting & Instructional Technology

 

Since September 2016, I have been a teaching consultant with the Center for Research on Learning and Teaching (CRLT). I have two types of positions with CRLT:

  1. Graduate Student Instructor Consultant (GSIC): In addition to our monthly consulting circles/meetings, I also perform personal consultations. Each semester I work with graduate student instructors across various departments to help them with teaching and learning in their classroom. This type of consultation is called midterm student feedback or MSF and has four stages. First, a pre-meeting with the instructor to see how things in their classroom are going. Second, a classroom observation and chat with the students about their learning. Three, creation of a formalized report based on the data and observations I had in the classroom visit. And fourth, a reflective post-meeting where I present the instructor with the formal report and provide a space to think about their classroom moving forward for the rest of the semester.
    • Bosses: Dr. Nicole Tuttle & Dr. Stephanie Moody
  2. Instructrional Technologist: Working with my colleagues, I help to develop and teaching workshop on integrating technology, informed by pedagogy, into the classrooms of University of Michigan instructors. I help to host one workshop per semester and a few workshops during the Enriching Scholarship week that happens during the first week in May every year at U of M.
    • Boss: Dr. Ronit Ajilen.

As an instructional technologist GSIC with CRLT, I have helped to develop and teach 5 workshops related to teaching and technology, including:

  1. “Teaching with Technology: Incorporating Digital Media”
  2. “Everyday Technology in Teaching”
  3. “Let’s make a screencast with Jing”
  4. “Teaching with Technology: How can I include all students?”
  5. “Canvas for Graduate Student Instructors”
